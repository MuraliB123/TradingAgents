## Fundamental Report for **KAYNES.NS**  
**Company:** Kaynes Technology India Limited  
**Sector:** Technology  
**Industry:** Electronic Components  
**Data timestamp:** 2026-05-28

### 1) Executive overview
**KAYNES.NS** remains a strong growth story with rapid revenue expansion, improving gross profitability, a manageable debt load on an EBITDA basis, and a healthy current ratio. However, the business is in a heavy investment phase: free cash flow is deeply negative, operating cash flow has weakened sharply, receivables and inventory have risen, and the company has funded growth partly through share issuance.  

From a trader’s perspective, the key question is not whether growth exists — it clearly does — but whether **KAYNES.NS can convert that growth into sustained cash generation without continued dilution or balance-sheet strain**.

### 5) Cash flow analysis

#### Annual cash flow trend
For **FY26**:
- **Operating cash flow:** **-₹6.00bn**
- **Capital expenditure:** **-₹12.40bn**
- **Free cash flow:** **-₹18.41bn**
- **Financing cash flow:** **+₹15.80bn**
- **End cash position:** ₹1.09bn

#### Key observations
1. **Free cash flow is deeply negative**
   - This is the biggest fundamental flag in the current report.
   - It reflects both:
     - heavy capex,
     - and weak operating cash conversion.

2. **Operating cash flow deteriorated sharply**
   - FY24 was slightly positive.
   - FY25 turned negative.
   - FY26 became much more negative.

3. **Financing covered the investment gap**
   - Cash inflows from financing were large.
   - Equity issuance alone was significant, which is important for dilution awareness.

#### Why this matters
A high-growth company can absolutely have negative FCF for a period, but the market usually rewards that only if:
- revenue keeps scaling,
- margins hold up,
- and the new assets produce stronger cash generation later.

For **KAYNES.NS**, the current question is whether the capex cycle is nearing the point where cash flow inflects upward.
